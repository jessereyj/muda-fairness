CHAPTER 6
DISCUSSION

6.1 Overview

In this chapter, the result from Scenario 1 is interpreted against the fairness properties in Chapter 4. The goal is to interpret the result and understand its significance. No new proofs are introduced.

From Scenario 1, a deterministic execution trace sigma is obtained starting from the initial state x0. The execution trace is obtained through repeated application of the transition function delta. In Phase P7 of the execution, the principle of stuttering applies. At this stage, the last state is repeated indefinitely, making the trace infinitely long.

At every point on the execution trace, there is a corresponding state in the protocol. Atomic propositions are evaluated at every point on the execution trace. This connects the operational semantics of the protocol in Chapter 3 with the LTL semantics in Chapter 4.

6.2 Priority Fairness Interpretation

From the analysis, it can be seen that priority fairness holds at all steps of the matching process. Whenever Phase P3 is reached, the algorithm chooses the most prioritized buyer and seller. There is no skipping of any priority level during this process.

This means that the greedy matching criterion is applied consistently within the model. Since the choice criterion remains constant at all steps, the execution does not make arbitrary decisions.

This property applies only to the matching stage. In phases other than Phase P3, no selection is performed. The protocol updates its state information or moves to the next phase.

6.3 Quantity Fairness Interpretation

The data show that quantity fairness holds at each stage of the algorithm. For each trade, there is an equality between the initial quantity and the sum of the allocated and residual quantities.

Thus, it follows that the accounting relation is correct. After each matching stage, the quantity changes, and the residual quantity indicates how many units remain. The relation between them remains constant.

After the terminal phase, stuttering preserves this relation. Since the state no longer changes, the accounting identity holds at all subsequent positions in the trace.

Therefore, the system is internally consistent.

6.4 Price Fairness Interpretation

The clearing price complies with the pricing formula whenever it is defined. This means the price lies between the marginal bid and ask and follows the deterministic formula.

At the beginning of the auction process, the clearing price is not defined since no matches exist yet. The condition therefore does not apply.
In the mechanization, the Chapter 4 atom for “clearing price exists” is defined via the computed function determine_clearing_price on the current match record, so it becomes true as soon as the match record is non-empty.
Separately, the state field clearing_price is populated by the pricing transition (Phase P4) and is carried from the subsequent Phase P5 state onward.
From the point where the computed clearing price is defined, the pricing formula is satisfied.

This shows that the pricing scheme is consistent with the matching result. The clearing price depends on the match record and does not change after the execution terminates.

6.5 Implications

This analysis indicates that fairness can be stated as invariants on execution sequences. Each property holds at every point in the sequence, as proven in Theorems 1-3 of Chapter 4.

The use of LTL provides a consistent formulation for these properties. The G φ formulation ensures that correctness is maintained throughout the entire process.

Stuttering allows finite sequences to be treated as infinite sequences. As a result, the sequence becomes suitable for LTL semantics.

Lifting maps state-level predicates to properties evaluated over the entire sequence. Thus, the definitions in Chapter 3 apply directly to the temporal framework in Chapter 4.

Taken together, these elements provide a method for proving fairness.

6.6 Limitations

In this chapter, only one particular case is considered. It provides an illustration of how the properties work in practice but does not establish their correctness in general. The general proofs are given in Chapter 4.

Only three properties of the system are considered. Properties such as maximality and justified rejection are outside the scope of this work.

Only invariants of the form G φ are used for temporal specification. Other temporal properties are not considered here.

6.7 Recommendations for Future Research

Future research may expand the list of verified properties. Other fairness conditions can also be stated using the model.

Other temporal properties apart from invariants may be explored. Termination and phase transition are examples. These require other LTL modalities.

Another direction for future research is to model more scenarios. This would help in understanding more execution behaviors of the system.

Finally, the proposed model can serve as a reference model for implementation. System executions can be compared with the model to verify their correctness.
