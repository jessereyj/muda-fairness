# Executive Summary (MUDA Fairness Thesis)

## What problem this solves
Multi-Unit Double Auctions (MUDA) are widely used to clear a batch of buy bids and sell asks for a homogeneous good at a *single uniform price*. Economic literature typically analyzes equilibrium/truthfulness properties, but does not provide a step-by-step *state-based execution model* suitable for proving execution-level fairness over time.

## Core idea
1. Model MUDA as a **deterministic state-transition system (STS)** with seven protocol phases (P1–P7).
2. Turn operational facts about a state (feasibility, priority choice, residual quantities, match record, clearing price, rejection) into **atomic propositions**.
3. Interpret full executions as **infinite traces** by **stuttering at the terminal state** (P7 is absorbing).
4. Express fairness as **LTL properties over the whole execution trace** using `G` (always), `F` (eventually), and `U` (until).
5. Mechanize the model and proofs in **Rocq (Coq)**, with fairness theorems proved using `Qed` (no `Admitted`).

## What is verified
For every execution trace generated by the deterministic transition function, the model satisfies six fairness properties:
- **Priority Fairness**: each greedy match step respects lexicographic price/time priority (buyers) and price/time priority (sellers).
- **Quantity Fairness**: residual quantities and allocations remain consistent; trades never allocate more than initial quantity.
- **Price Fairness**: if a clearing price exists, it follows the uniform-price rule and is bounded by the marginal ask/bid.
- **Match Finality**: after matching ends, the match record is never modified.
- **Maximality**: once the protocol enters post-matching phases (P4–P7), there are no feasible buyer–seller pairs left.
- **Justified Rejection**: any rejected agent has no feasible trading partner at the post-matching boundary.

## Why LTL (and why stuttering)
Fairness is about *persistence across steps* (e.g., “priority is respected at every match step”) and *end-of-execution conditions* (e.g., “after matching, no feasible pairs remain”). LTL is a natural fit.

The model extends finite executions to infinite traces by stuttering at P7. The key point: the fairness formulas are written so that stuttering does not change their truth in the trace (the repo’s fairness checks are semantic satisfaction over traces).

## What is assumed vs proved (in the repo)
- **Alignment note**: the mechanization (and `docs/thesis.txt`) states Price Fairness as an implication `G(has_cprice → (bounds ∧ rule))` so executions with no trades (hence no clearing price) are not excluded.
- **Proved (constructively)**: MUDA phase behavior, match-record properties, clearing-price boundedness, and the six fairness theorems as LTL satisfaction statements.
- **Axiomatized (explicit)**: correctness of the sorting operators and that greedy matching respects the priority ordering (documented in `README.md`).
